Courses

Healthy Minds offer a range of courses for stress control, living live to the full, emotional skills, mindfullness for health and others.

To complete one of the courses, please complete this form. You can see the full breakdown of each course here or call Healthy Minds on 0161 716 2777 to find out more.

Stress control

Stress is your body’s reaction to feeling under pressure and is very common. In fact, four out of 10 people say that stress is a big problem for them and it is one of the most common reasons that people see their GP.

  • Week 1: Meet and greet – An introduction to the course. Between each session, you're expected to go away and practice the techniques we have looked at. You'll be given any materials needed to help with this.
  • Week 2: What is stress?
  • Week 3: Anxiety and the body
  • Week 4: Anxiety and our thoughts
  • Week 5: Anxiety and our actions
  • Week 6: Anxiety and panic
  • Week 7: Anxiety, depression and sleep

Living life to the fullest

Changes in our mood are a normal part of life for most of us. Four out of 10 people say that low mood can be a big problem for them, and it is one of the most common reasons that people see their GP.

  • Week 1: Meet and greet - meet the course leaders and the rest of the group in a relaxed manner, learn more about the course and how to start making some changes. You can bring a friend or family member along to this session to support you.
  • Week 2: Why do I feel so bad? – introducing the vicious cycle of low mood.
  • Week 3: I can’t be bothered doing anything – increasing activity and improving motivation.
  • Week 4: Why does everything go wrong? – low mood and our thoughts.
  • Week 5: I’m not good enough – low mood and low self esteem.
  • Week 6: How to fix almost anything – problem solving.
  • Week 7: The things you do that mess you up – things we are doing which can sabotage change.
  • Week 8: Are you strong enough to keep your temper? – Managing anger and irritability.
  • 10 things that make you feel happier straight away – relapse prevention.

Mindfulness for health

The mindfulness for health course provides you with a wide range of mindfulness skills to ease the suffering associated with persistent pain, fatigue and long term health conditions (including stress and low mood that often accompanies such conditions).

  • Week 1: Meet and greet – An introduction to the course. Between each session, you're expected to go away and practice the techniques we have looked at. You'll be given any materials needed to help with this.
  • Breath awareness: Learn to use the natural breath as an aid to managing our pain, illness or stress.
  • Body awareness: The body scan helps us to develop habits of greater ease and awareness of the body.
  • Mindful movement: We guide you through some gentle movements, based on yoga and pilates, especially devised by a very experienced yoga teacher.
  • Mindfulness of daily life: Learn to overcome the very common tendency of overdoing it on good days or collapsing on bad days.
  • Meditation practices: Seven different mindfulness meditations. Body scan; breathing anchor; mindful movement; acceptance and self-compassion; the treasure of pleasure meditation; open heart meditation; connection meditation.
  • Home practice: This will take at least 20-30 minutes a day, six days a week, for eight weeks, and involves listening to the recorded meditations and other exercises, performing brief exercises, and so on.
